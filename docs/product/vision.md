# Vision

## The North Star

> **"A product so good, people tell their friends."**

This is the single test every feature must pass. Not: does this add value? But: is this good enough that someone would recommend it unprompted?

The second constraint that follows directly from this: **we never build vitamins.** Vitamins are nice-to-have — users take them when they remember, cancel when money is tight. Painkillers are used because not using them hurts. pondex must be a painkiller — indispensable, not optional.

Every Council review, every feature decision, every UX tradeoff is held against these two questions:
1. Is this good enough that someone tells a friend?
2. Does this solve a real pain — or is it a vitamin?

---

## The One Sentence

pondex helps self-directed retail investors make better investment decisions — not by showing more data, but by providing context, structure, and a system that thinks alongside them.

---

## The Problem (Problem Space — no solutions here)

### P1 — Data Without Context
Yahoo Finance, Morningstar, and Seeking Alpha show numbers. P/E 28x. Gross margin 61%. Revenue growth 18%. But what does that mean? Is a P/E of 28x cheap or expensive for this company, in this sector, with this growth rate? No free tool answers that. They display — they don't interpret.

### P2 — Strategy Blindness
No free tool asks who you are as an investor. The same stock gets the same score regardless of whether you're a value investor with a 10-year horizon or a momentum trader. That's wrong. The same stock is genuinely different for different strategies.

### P3 — Research Fragmentation
Analyzing a stock means opening five tabs, reading contradictory opinions, and making a gut decision after two hours. The information exists — it's just scattered, unfiltered, and disconnected from any personal context.

### P4 — The Discovery Gap
You analyze NVDA because you know NVDA. The European semiconductor supplier trading 20% below fair value, perfectly aligned with your profile, never gets analyzed — because you never heard of it.

---

## Why Now

AI inference is cheap enough to run meaningful analysis on free-tier APIs. Browser storage is powerful enough to run a complete portfolio system client-side. The tools to build this have matured — the product hasn't been built yet.

---

## What Success Looks Like (in 3 years)

A retail investor opens pondex, enters a ticker, and understands within two minutes whether this stock fits their strategy — and why. They document their thesis at the time of purchase. Three months later, pondex tells them whether it still holds. After one year, they know where they systematically make mistakes and where their real edge is.

They don't need five tabs. They don't need a financial advisor. They make better decisions — and they can prove it.

## What pondex Is Not (today)

- Not a social network. No comments, no copy trading, no trending stocks.
- Not financial advice. Algorithmic signals, not recommendations.
- Not designed to maximize engagement. No gamification, no streaks, no push bombardment.

## Where This Is Going — The Long Vision

Revolut started as a travel money card. It took them 6 years to become a full bank.
pondex starts as a research tool. The path to something larger is earned — not assumed.

Every phase below must be validated before the next begins. Nothing gets built on hope.

### The Journey

**Today — Research Layer**
The noise filter. You enter a ticker, you get a verdict with sources. Every broker's users can use it. Broker-agnostic is the feature.

**Year 1–2 — Decision Intelligence Platform**
Thesis tracking. Exit signals. Portfolio calendar. You don't just research before buying — pondex stays with you during holding and tells you when your thesis breaks. This is where switching costs compound. After 12 months of theses logged, leaving pondex means losing your own investment history.

**Year 2–3 — Personal Finance Intelligence**
pondex knows your portfolio, your strategy, your goals, your track record. It starts surfacing opportunities you didn't search for — stocks that match your proven decision patterns, not generic screener results. The Discovery Gap (P4) finally gets solved.

**Year 3–4 — Broker Partnerships (White-Label)**
pondex doesn't need a broker licence to integrate with brokers. A "Buy via [Trade Republic / IBKR / Comdirect]" button sends users to their existing broker with affiliate commission. pondex earns per trade without holding a single euro of client money. This is the business model expansion — research-to-execution without regulatory overhead.

**Year 4–5 — Licensed Investment Platform (if validated)**
If broker affiliate data shows users want to execute inside pondex — not just click out — then the MiFID II licence conversation begins. This requires: investor capital (€500k–€1M minimum), a compliance team, and proof that users trust pondex with real money. Revolut had 1M users before they applied for a banking licence. The sequence matters.

**Year 5+ — The Full Stack**
At this point pondex is what Revolut is — but built from the research side, not the payments side. Research → Decision → Execution → Portfolio → Tax. The difference: every user who arrives came because they trusted the analysis. That trust is the moat no bank can replicate by adding a research tab.

## The Revolut Comparison — Honestly

Revolut built trust through payments, then added investing.
pondex builds trust through research, then adds execution.

Both end up in the same place. The paths are different. The advantage of the pondex path: users who come for research are already engaged with their investments. They're not passive savers — they're active decision-makers. That's a better starting user for a long-term financial platform.

Revolut has 45M users and a banking licence. pondex has 0 users and a research tool.
The gap is real. The path is clear. Start with the thing that's true today:
**source-cited research that no one else provides, at a price point no competitor offers, for a market that is explicitly underserved.**

Everything else is earned from there.
