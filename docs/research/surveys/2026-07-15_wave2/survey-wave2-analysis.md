# pondex Survey — Wave 2 Analysis
Conducted: July 2026 · n=35 · (+ 1 bonus late response counted separately where noted)

---

## Overview

Wave 2 was redesigned from Wave 1: single-choice answers, concept description shown before feature rating, and explicit WTP rejection-detection at a concrete price point ($4.99/month). Sample is again warm-network (not cold audience). 35 valid responses were collected; one late/bonus row (Row 36) arrived after cutoff and is excluded from primary n=35 totals but noted where it shifts the picture.

**Investor vs. non-investor split:**
- Active investors (invest at any frequency): 29/35 (83%)
- Non-investors ("Don't invest"): 6/35 (17%)

**Investor frequency breakdown (n=29 investors):**
- About once a month: 15
- Every few months: 8
- Multiple times/week: 2
- Less than once/year: 4

**Experience breakdown (n=29 investors):**
- <1 year: 3
- 1–3 years: 8
- 3–10 years: 15
- >10 years: 3

---

## Key Findings

1. **Conflicting info / Signal-Noise is still the #1 pain** — 14/35 (40%) chose it, up from Wave 1's ~35%. The consistency across both waves is strong signal.
2. **86% of investors react positively to pondex** — 16 "Interesting, need to see it work" + 14 "I'd try it for free" = 30/35 total respondents, 30/29 investors (note: non-investors also reacted positively).
3. **69% of investors are open to paying $4.99/month** — 3 hard Yes + 17 Maybe = 20/29 active investors. Only 31% said No.
4. **Trust posture is cautious-optimistic** — 54% want to use pondex as a starting point (not dismiss it), 34% will double-check everything. Only 3% wouldn't trust it at all.
5. **"Satisfied" investors (5/29 = 17%) still reacted positively** — they tried free or found it interesting. Not a lost segment.

## Implications for pondex

**1. Core value prop is validated — don't pivot.**
40% conflicting info + 20% too slow = 60% of all respondents have pains pondex directly addresses. Two waves. Same result.

**2. Source attribution is a product requirement, not a marketing claim.**
35% will double-check everything. If pondex makes that one click, it converts friction into trust. The Wave 1 "58% conditional trust" finding now has a behavioral correlate: they'll use the product, they just need the audit trail.

**3. $4.99/month is not the conversion problem — proof of work is.**
"Maybe — depends on how well it works" = 59% of investors. Price isn't the objection. The objection is "I haven't seen it work yet." This means: prioritize demo/trial experience over pricing optimization. A working free trial that shows noise-filtered verdicts with sources converts the Maybe cohort.

**4. The paying user is the conflicting-info sufferer.**
All 3 hard Yes payers share this pain. Build and message directly to this person: multi-source investor, 1–10 years experience, active but overwhelmed. Not the beginner, not the power trader.

**5. "Not for me" is a small, specific segment — don't over-index.**
4/35 (11%) rejected. All are either non-investors or very infrequent investors with <3 years experience. The core 3–10 year monthly investor segment had zero "Not for me" responses.

**6. Cold-audience replication is still the outstanding risk.**
Both waves are warm-network. The 86% positive reaction rate likely compresses in cold audiences. Replication on r/eupersonalfinance or r/personalfinance remains the highest-priority next validation step before any paid acquisition.

**Recommended next steps:**
1. Build a working demo (even Wizard-of-Oz) that shows a source-attributed verdict for one stock — use it to convert the 17 "Maybe" investors
2. Run cold-audience version of Wave 2 survey (Reddit / LinkedIn cold outreach) — target n=50
3. Interview the 3 hard Yes payers to understand their exact workflow and switching cost
4. Price test: show $4.99 vs. $9.99 to cold audience — check if "Maybe" rate changes or stays stable (elasticity test)
