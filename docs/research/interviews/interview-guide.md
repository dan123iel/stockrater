## The 3 things you MUST learn from each interview

1. **What does their research process look like step by step today?** (not what they wish — what they actually do)
2. **Where exactly does it break down?** (the moment of frustration — be specific)
3. **Would they pay for pondex, and what would make them stay?** (for Gunnar: what made him leave)

Everything else is bonus.

> **Recommended interview order (added 2026-07-09):** Patricia → Bernardo → Gunnar.

### Cheat Sheet 001 — Gunnar Leu
**Segment:** Churner · Value investor · paid $15–50/mo
**Focus:** Cancellation moment + what would have made him stay + pricing signal

**A — Opening (30 sec)**
> *"Thanks for making time. Quick ground rules: I'm not pitching anything today — I'm here to understand how you actually work. No wrong answers. I'll only use the recording for my own notes and delete it afterwards — can I record the call?"*

Shut up. Let him settle.

**B — Warm-up (5 min) — his world today**
1. *"Walk me through the last time you researched a stock — step by step, from the moment you decided to look at it."*
2. *"Which tools did you open? In what order?"*
3. *"How long until you felt confident enough to make a call — or did you just decide anyway?"*

Listen for: which tools, where he switches, whether confidence ever arrives.

**C — Pain (8 min)**
4. *"What's the most frustrating part of that process?"*
5. *"How often does that happen?"*
6. *"What have you tried to fix it?"*

5-Why follow-ups (pick the most loaded answer):
- *"Why is that frustrating?"*
- *"What does it cost you — time, money, confidence?"*
- *"What happens when it goes wrong?"*

Count to 5 before jumping in if he pauses.

**D — Churner-specific (10 min)**
7. *"You paid for a research tool and then cancelled. What was the exact moment you decided to stop — was there a specific thing that happened?"*
8. *"What would the tool have needed to do differently for you to stay?"*
9. *"You mentioned geopolitics + fundamentals + charts in one place. Is that still the core thing, or has that changed?"*

   → *"If the tool had everything except geopolitics — would you still use it, or is that a dealbreaker?"*

10. *"If you had that — what does a perfect research session look like for you? Walk me through it."*

**E — Product reaction (3 min)**

Paste this — do NOT demo:
> *"Here's what we're building — text only, nothing visual yet: You enter a stock ticker. In under 60 seconds you get a plain-language verdict: 'This stock is fairly valued — here's why.' Every number shows exactly where it comes from: Yahoo Finance, SEC filings. An AI explains each factor before showing the score. What's your reaction?"*

- *"What's missing from this?"*
- *"What would make you not use it?"*
- *"What would make you tell a friend about it?"*

**F — Pricing (critical — do not anchor)**
> *"The tool I'm describing gives you a plain-language verdict in 60 seconds, with every number and its source. If that solved your problem — what would you pay per month?"*

Shut up. Wait as long as it takes. Do not suggest a number.

**G — Van Westendorp (exact order)**
1. *"At what monthly price would this be so expensive you'd never consider it?"*
2. *"At what price would it start to seem expensive, but you might still consider it?"*
3. *"At what price would it seem like a good deal?"*
4. *"At what price would it be so cheap you'd question the quality?"*

Write down exact numbers.

**H — Wrap-up (2 min)**
- *"What should I have asked you that I didn't?"*
- *"Do you know anyone else with this problem?"*
- *"Can I reach out when we have a beta to test?"*

## Universal Structure (all 3 interviews)

### 1. Opening — 2 min

> *"Thanks for making time. Quick ground rules: I'm not pitching you anything today — I'm here to understand how you actually work. There are no wrong answers. The more honest you are, the more useful this is. Can I record the call?"*

Then shut up. Let them settle.

---

### 2. Warm-up: Their world today — 5 min

Ask these in order. **Do not skip to the next question until they've fully answered.**

1. *"Walk me through the last time you researched a stock. What did you do, step by step?"*
2. *"Which tools did you open? In what order?"*
3. *"How long did it take until you felt confident enough to decide?"*

**What to listen for:**
- Which tools (Yahoo Finance, Seeking Alpha, Google, Reddit, ChatGPT?)
- Where they stop and switch tools — that's the friction point
- Whether they ever feel confident or just give up and decide anyway
