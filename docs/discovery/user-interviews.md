## Before the interviews — prep checklist

- [ ] Quiet room, no interruptions, headphones in
- [ ] Notion / paper ready for notes — do NOT type during the call, it kills the conversation. Voice memo or Zoom recording instead, transcribe after.
- [ ] Ask for recording permission in the first 30 seconds
- [ ] Have the product description text ready to paste (see below)
- [ ] Do NOT open pondex or demo anything — text description only
- [ ] Have the Van Westendorp pricing questions ready (see below)

---

## The 3 things you MUST learn from each interview

1. **What does their research process look like step by step today?** (not what they wish — what they actually do)
2. **Where exactly does it break down?** (the moment of frustration — be specific)
3. **Would they pay for pondex, and what would make them stay?** (for Gunnar: what made him leave)

Everything else is bonus.

---

## Universal Structure (all 3 interviews)

### 1. Opening — 2 min

> *"Thanks for making time. Quick ground rules: I'm not pitching you anything today — I'm here to understand how you actually work. There are no wrong answers. The more honest you are, the more useful this is. Can I record the call?"*

Then shut up. Let them settle.

---

### 2. Warm-up: Their world today — 5 min

Ask these in order. **Do not skip to the next question until they've fully answered.**

1. *"Walk me through the last time you researched a stock. What did you do, step by step?"*
2. *"Which tools did you open? In what order?"*
3. *"How long did it take until you felt confident enough to decide?"*

**What to listen for:**
- Which tools (Yahoo Finance, Seeking Alpha, Google, Reddit, ChatGPT?)
- Where they stop and switch tools — that's the friction point
- Whether they ever feel confident or just give up and decide anyway

---

### 3. The pain — 8 min

4. *"What's the most frustrating part of that process?"*
5. *"How often does that happen?"*
6. *"What have you tried to fix it?"*

**Then go deeper with 5-Whys:**
- *"Why is that frustrating?"*
- *"What happens when it goes wrong?"*
- *"What does that cost you — in time, money, confidence?"*

**Do not suggest answers.** If they pause, wait. Count to 5 in your head before jumping in.

---

### 4. Profile-specific questions

#### Gunnar Leu (Churner)
7. *"You mentioned you paid for a research tool and then cancelled. What was the moment you decided to stop?"*
8. *"What would the tool have needed to do differently for you to stay?"*
9. *"You mentioned geopolitics + fundamentals + charts in one place. Is that still the most important thing for you, or has that changed?"*
10. *"If you had that tool today — what would a perfect research session look like?"*

**Critical question for pricing:**
> *"The tool I'm building gives a plain-language verdict on any stock in 60 seconds, with every number showing where it comes from. If that solved your problem — what would you pay per month?"*
> — Then be quiet. Do not anchor with a number first.

---

#### José Bernardo S. (Passive investor — "Everything in one place")
7. *"When you say 'everything in one place' — what does 'everything' mean to you specifically?"*
8. *"Today you use multiple tools — which one do you trust most? Why?"*
9. *"What would have to happen for you to close all the other tabs and just use one thing?"*
10. *"How often do you actually research stocks — daily, weekly, monthly?"*

**Critical question:**
> *"If a tool gave you a clear verdict in plain language with sources — would that replace your current process, or just add to it?"*

---

#### Patricia G. Parnet (Passive investor — "Daily overview + annual reports")
7. *"You mentioned wanting a daily overview — what does that look like in your head? What would you see?"*
8. *"Annual reports — do you read them yourself or are you looking for someone to interpret them for you?"*
9. *"How do you decide when to look at a stock more closely versus ignoring it?"*
10. *"What does your ideal Monday morning routine look like if you had the perfect tool?"*

**Critical question:**
> *"Is this something you'd use every day, or only when you're about to make a decision?"*
> — This tells you if it's a habit or a research tool.

---

### 5. Product reaction — 3 min

**Paste this text — do NOT demo the live product yet:**

> *"Here's what we're building — just a description, nothing visual yet:*
>
> *You enter a stock ticker. In under 60 seconds you get a plain-language verdict: 'This stock is fairly valued — here's why.' Every number shows exactly where it comes from: Yahoo Finance, SEC filings. An AI explains each factor in plain language before showing you the score.*
>
> *What's your reaction?"*

Then ask:
- *"What's missing from this?"*
- *"What would make you not use it?"*
- *"What would make you tell a friend about it?"* ← North Star question

---

### 6. Wrap-up — 2 min

- *"What should I have asked you that I didn't?"*
- *"Is there anyone else you know who has this problem?"* ← referral signal
- *"Can I reach out when we have a beta version to test?"*

---

## Van Westendorp Pricing (ask all 3, at the end)

Only after you've shown the product description. Ask in this exact order — do not skip or reorder:

1. *"At what monthly price would this be so expensive you'd never consider it?"*
2. *"At what price would it start to seem expensive, but you might still consider it?"*
3. *"At what price would it seem like a good deal?"*
4. *"At what price would it be so cheap you'd question the quality?"*

Write down exact numbers — don't round them.
