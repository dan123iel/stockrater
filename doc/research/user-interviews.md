## Interview Guide

**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Format:** Video call or async written responses  
**Recruitment criteria:** Self-directed investor, owns individual stocks, 25–45 years old

### Questions

**Context**
1. How long have you been investing in individual stocks?
2. Roughly how many positions do you hold?
3. What's your typical investment horizon?

**Research workflow**
4. Walk me through your last stock purchase decision. What did you do?
5. How many sources do you typically consult before buying?
6. What question do you most often leave unanswered after researching a stock?
7. Have you ever regretted a decision because you didn't have a piece of information?

**Decision documentation**
8. Do you write down why you buy a stock? If yes, where?
9. Have you ever forgotten why you bought something? What happened?
10. If a tool reminded you of your original thesis 6 months later, would that be useful?

**Discovery**
11. How do you discover new stocks to look at?
12. Have you ever bought something you found through a tool recommendation (not media)?

**Tools**
13. What tools do you use today? What do you pay for?
14. What frustrates you most about your current setup?
15. Is there a tool you use that you genuinely enjoy? What makes it good?
